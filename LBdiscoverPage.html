Dabke

Across Lebanon, a popular activity and tradition people participate in is Dabke. Dabke is a dance that is performed in the Levantine region of the Middle East. Dabke is commonly seen at parties and weddings or traditions. The Dabke dance is an exciting dance as it is meant to be fun for everyone watching or participating. Sellman says, “The Dabke is a dance where everyone stands in a line holding hands facing outwards or to the audience, if there is an audience. There are many versions of dabke, but the most common one is when the dancers step with the left foot and right foot and then cross the left foot and right foot over. These steps require a small hop as they are done”. The dance is a unifying dance as others can join in and dance together. Additionally, Dabke is often danced to with slow music transitioning to fast music. The excitement and fun that the dance always brings happiness and joyous feelings towards others. Sellman also states, “Because dabke is a unifying dance, you will always see people randomly joining (breaking a pair's hands to make room for themselves to join) or someone trying to get other members to join.” In more emphasis this iconic piece of Lebanese culture is a fun activity for tourists and for everyone to enjoy together. As Badaoui explains, “Today, it is a vital part of any Lebanese celebration. Whenever family and friends gather, the dabke music fills the air and everyone rushes to grab hands, and, shoulders to shoulders, stamp with the rhythm on the dance floor!” Since this dance is a vital part of celebrations in Lebanon, it can be performed in areas where there are audiences. In which, tourists can enjoy spending time dancing in synchronised fashion to music with others. Moreover, the Dabke dance, is recommended for tourists to participate in to embrace the culture and tradition in Lebanon.

Pidgeon Rock

An iconic and unforgettable site in Lebanon for tourists to see is Pigeon Rock or Raouche rock. Pigeon Rock is located in Beirut, the capital of Lebanon and it is close to the West Beirut Sea. Sameh states, “The Raouche Rock is located in Lebanon, which is close to the West Beirut Sea, where it consists of two large rocks located in the middle of the Mediterranean, close to the coast of the Raouche region, and is the first landmark a tourist sees on the plane before landing on the land of Rafic Hariri International Airport in the capital Beirut. . . It is also considered an important maritime tourist landmark in Lebanon, and a magnet for many tourists to enjoy watching and practicing a lot of tourism activities in it.” On the other hand, Pigeon Rock is an important natural tourist destination to visit, as Pigeon Rock has characteristics that make it so beautiful to see, also Pigeon Rock is a wonderful tourist attraction to visit, as many people come to this site to spend time, and enjoy practicing tourism activities. Sameh explains, “Some scholars specializing in geology refer to the origin of the emergence of this large rock as a result of several earthquakes that struck this part of the Beirut Sea. It led to the elimination of many islands that were in this populated area, and many rocks appeared among them, including the Raouche Rock, and this is due to The thirteenth century AD.” The history of Pigeon Rock is unique and is interesting to learn about how the beautiful rock formation was created. Pigeon Rock makes for a great tourist attraction as tourists can take a boat ride around Pigeon Rock and through them too to get a beautiful view and sight of the rock formations. Pigeon Rock makes for a great site for tourists to visit, as it represents the beauty of Lebanon.
