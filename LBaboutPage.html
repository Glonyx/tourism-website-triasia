About Lebanon

Lebanon is a remarkable country as it contains some of the most interesting historical landmarks, beautiful cities, tourist attractions, beautiful natural locations, and so much more. Lebanon is a country that is located on the eastern shore of the Mediterranean Sea, it is located between Syria to the north and east and Israel to the south, and its capital is Beirut. As a major city in Lebanon, Beirut has some of the most ancient attractions that still exist in the modern world today. Some of these attractions include Pigeon Rocks, Jeita Grotto, and more. Throughout Lebanon, there are a lot of ancient locations, and ancient history available to be seen. Britannica states, “Though Lebanon, particularly its coastal region, was the site of some of the oldest human settlements in the world—the Phoenician ports of Tyre (modern Ṣūr), Sidon (Ṣaydā), and Byblos (Jubayl) were dominant centres of trade and culture in the 3rd millennium BCE—it was not until 1920 that the contemporary state came into being. In that year France, which administered Lebanon as a League of Nations mandate, established the state of Greater Lebanon. Lebanon then became a republic in 1926 and achieved independence in 1943.” The history of Lebanon is fascinating as settlements that existed far back into the past can still be seen today. The country of Lebanon has cultural and demographic aspects that make it much different and diverse from any other country in the world. According to Britannica, “Historically, Lebanon is heir to a long succession of Mediterranean cultures—Phoenician, Greek, and Arab. Its cultural milieu continues to show clear manifestations of a rich and diverse heritage.

As an Arab country, Lebanon shares more than a common language with neighbouring Arab states: it also has a similar cultural heritage and common interests.” Throughout Lebanon, there are a multitude of religions and cultures that the Lebanese follow through their daily lives. The Lebanese live on a healthy diet and are mostly characterized by culture. A typical Lebanese cuisine consists of herbs, vegetables. Fruit, olives, olive oil, laban(yogurt), rice, and khubz (flatbread). With Lebanon having such diverse cultures, there are demographics that indicate what the population consists of and also, what the conditions are like in Lebanon. Britannica states that, “Lebanon’s birth rate is slightly below the world’s average, while its death rate is well below the global average. About one-fourth of the population is under age 15, with more than one-half under age 30. Life expectancy in Lebanon is higher than both the regional and world averages. One of the most salient demographic features of Lebanon is the uneven distribution of its population. The country’s overall population density varies regionally and is on the whole much lower than that of Beirut and the surrounding area but much higher than that of the most sparsely populated Al-Biqāʿ valley.”

Religion is an integral part of Lebanon, as religions vary throughout the country, which gives the country an interesting aspect to look at. Lebanon is characterized by its vast amount of religions and cultural aspects, the natural locations and major cities make Lebanon an extraordinary country. According to Culture Trip, “Located in Bsharri, a district in Northern Lebanon, the Cedars is one of Lebanon’s prized treasures. Famous for its legendary cedar trees, the area is a tourist destination as ski resort in the winter and hiking location in the summer. What makes the trees special is their place in human history. They were part of the forest in the Epic of Gilgamesh, utilized by King Solomon to build a temple in Jerusalem and used to build the Phoenicians historical fleet.” The history of Lebanon’s beautiful Cedar of God forest captivates and shows just what a beautiful country Lebanon is. However, there are other natural locations that make Lebanon what it is today. Culture Trip states, “Located between Bcharre and Zgharta, the Kadisha Valley is a UNESCO World Heritage site carved by the Kadisha River. Also known as the Holy Valley, the stretch of land is known for having been a site for worship since the early days of Christianity and has seen human activity since the Paleolithic period. Its proximity to the Cedar forest, or what’s left of the legendary Cedars, makes it a valley marked by spirituality and legend.” There are a multitude of natural locations that characterize Lebanon to be an ancient country that exists today. On par with old and important natural locations, there are major cities that are beautiful. World Atlas states, “Beirut is Lebanon’s capital, the largest and the most populous with a population of 1,916,100 people. The ancient city is the most important commercial port in Lebanon with commercial trading points, banks, and local and foreign businesses. Its rich cultural heritage has developed over centuries of interaction with different people from various countries and societies. The town attracts a huge number of tourists to the museums such as the National Museum of Beirut, the Sursock Museum, and the Archaeological Museum of the American University of Beirut.” The capital of Beirut is a major staple in Lebanon as it is the capital of the country. Beirut has so much to see as the city is right by the Mediterranean sea which provides a gorgeous view when looking at the city from afar.

Other than the capital of Beirut, Lebanon has other cities that make the country beautiful. According to World Atlas, “Tripoli is an ancient city with a great archaeological importance due to its rich architectural heritage that has developed throughout the centuries of its existence. Tripoli served as an important financial center and a port during the early centuries of its establishment and has remained a major port in Lebanon. The city has important tourist attractions such as the clock tower and the Palm Islands Nature Reserves. Tripoli is an important religious center with mosques and churches, educational center, and transport and communication center.” Tourists would appreciate everything that Lebanon has to offer. With such beautiful tourist attractions, interesting country history, and much more, Lebanon is a perfect country to visit that holds its place in the world.

Major Cities

Lebanon consists of many cities, however, the most notable cities in Lebanon are Beirut, Jounieh, and Byblos. Each of these cities have a significance to them that makes Lebanon one of the most beautiful countries for tourists to visit! Beirut contains beautiful tourist attractions such as Pigeon Rock, Jeita Grotto, Lady of Lebanon, and much more. The other major cities such as Jounieh contain beautiful sites to see and much more. Byblos is a remarkable city in Lebanon as it contains many ancient ruins and attractions to see across the country, as well as natural areas that tourists can visit in Byblos.
