Traveling and Transportation in Lebanon

While Lebanon is a country of beauty, there are questions tourists can ask about travelling to this Middle Eastern country. There are common travel options that tourists can take to get straight to Lebanon. Common travel options that tourists can take according to the World Travel Guide, “The national carrier is Middle East Airlines which operates direct flights from the UK to Beirut, as does British Airways. The peak summer months and Christmas generally tend to be the most expensive times to fly.” Plane is the most common form of international travel, in which tourists can travel to Lebanon. Generally flying is the more common mode of transportation for most tourists, however, other modes of travel are accessible. According to the World Travel Guide, “A few cruise ships visit Beirut in the summer. Thomson Cruises stop at Beirut on a cruise of the eastern Mediterranean departing from Cyprus.” Travelling to Lebanon by boat is optional, but offers an interesting travel method for tourists to try out.

Along with travelling to Lebanon, Lebanon offers in-country transportation and is used locally in the country and is easily accessible. According to iExplore, “Renting a car in Lebanon is possible, but most visitors choose to get one with a local driver. Travelers wanting to go independently will need to make this clear when ordering a vehicle as it is more uncommon. Most of the large cities have international car rental companies such as Avis, Hertz, Budget, and Europcar.” Renting a car is great for tourists to travel across Lebanon, however, their are other modes of transportation that are accessible within Lebanon iExplore states, “Shared taxis are one of the most common modes of getting around and between cities. Intercity taxis operate throughout the country and while most of them have set routes, there are no set prices so negotiate before departure. Shared taxis usually carry a maximum of six passengers on long journeys.” Tourists may find themselves comfortable with using a local service most easily accessible. Taxis are a safer option for tourists to opt in when travelling through towns and cities in Lebanon.

While there are common modes of transportation in Lebanon , there are also accommodations tourists can suit themselves with. There are many apartments, hotels, and many lodging options around the country that tourists can pick from. The Hotel Guru states, “The Phoenicia is the grand dame of Beirut’s hotel scene, a place that’s hosted all of the city’s most glamorous visitors since it opened in 1961. It’s big and lavish, with 446 rooms and really world class facilities and decor. It’s also in an excellent position, of course.” The Hotel Guru also states, “Le Vendome Beirut Hotel’s French name carries through into the decor of this 73 room hotel in a central spot with fabulous sea views. There’s no pool and spa, but guests have complimentary access to the pool and spa of the nearby The Phoenicia.” Throughout all of Lebanon, tourists can find many ways to get around the country especially with having many lodging options to choose from in the country. The many lodging options Lebanon has are incredible as they are culturally inspired and are comfortable to live in. There are many options tourists can pick from when travelling to Lebanon, in-country transportation, and accommodations that all tourists can enjoy from.
